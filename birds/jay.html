Eurasian Jay

松鴉 · 松鸦 · 松鴉 · Geai des chênes · Arrendajo común

Garrulus glandarius

A pinkish-brown crow with bright blue wing patches. Jays bury thousands of acorns each autumn — and forget where some are, helping new oak trees grow!

Size
Medium · 35 cm
Latin name
Garrulus glandarius
Status
Native to Ireland
Where to spot
  • Oak woodlands
  • Parks with mature trees
  • Killarney National Park

Where you can spot the Eurasian Jay in Ireland

  • Killarney National Park — County Kerry: Ancient oak woods and big lakes. Watch the sky for white-tailed eagles, brought back to Ireland from 2007. The oak woods buzz with songbirds.
