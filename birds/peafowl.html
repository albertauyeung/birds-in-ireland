Indian Peafowl

藍孔雀 · 蓝孔雀 · 藍孔雀 · Paon bleu · Pavo real común

Pavo cristatus

The famous peacock! The male's body is shimmering blue and his huge fan-tail is covered in shiny 'eyes'. They were brought to Ireland from India long ago and now strut around a few parks. Listen for their loud 'meow!' call.

Where you can spot the Indian Peafowl in Ireland

  • Marlay Park — Dublin (Rathfarnham): A grand parkland estate in south Dublin, once the country home of the Le Touche banking family. Free-roaming peacocks call from the trees near the walled garden, mallards paddle on the ponds, and the woodlands are full of garden birds. Free entry, popular with families at weekends.
